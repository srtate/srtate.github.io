How to check and grade NP-completeness proofs

On this page I’ll go through how I would grade a student-submitted NP-completeness proof. Let’s say it’s a 15 point problem, and so I’ll allocate points based on the basic proof components that we’ve discussed. Here’s a breakdown – go back through the example proof and see if you can identify the specific parts of the proof that correspond to these items:

1 Point: Are the parts of the proof easily identified or introduced (typically individual paragraphs), and is the proof properly concluded?

4 Points: Proof that the language is in NP. Of these 4 points, let’s break it down like this:

  • 2 points: Does the proof clearly state/define what the certificate is? Does it explain (or is it clear) that such a certificate exists if and only if the input is in the language?

  • 2 points: Does the proof clearly state what the verifier does, and why it’s polynomial time?

4 points: Definition/description of the reduction function. This part needs to identify a known NP-complete problem, and give a procedure for converting an input designed for that language into an input designed for the language we’re proving is NP-complete.

  • Overarching issue: Is the reduction done in the correct direction? It must covert an input for the known NP-complete problem into an input for the problem we’re interested in. If the proof starts with an input for our problem, then it’s backwards and pretty much nothing in the rest of the proof is useful.

  • 2 points: Is the reduction function clearly defined? It cannot make any assumptions about the input at all (that it is or is not in the language, for instance).

  • 2 points: Is it justified (or clear and just stated) that the reduction can be computed in polynomial time?

3 points: The “if” direction of the NP-completeness proof. In other words, if \(KNPC\) is the known NP-complete problem we’re using, and \(L\) is the language we’re trying to prove, this is the part of the proof that shows that “if \(x\in KNPC\) then \(f(x)\in L\)”.

3 points: The “only if” direction of the NP-completeness proof. In other words, this is the part of the proof that shows that “if \(f(x)\in L\) then \(x\in KNPC\)”.

Try taking one of your proofs and going through these points, and grade your work. Did you get everything? Does this identify areas you need to improve?
